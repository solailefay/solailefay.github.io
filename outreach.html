Field Outings & Mentorship

Taking people into the field

Getting communities on the ground

I believe deeply that ecological knowledge grows more meaningful when people experience wild places directly. Over the past three years I have led field outings for over 200 participants — from middle-school students encountering wildlife for the first time to community members learning about raptor ecology from the landscape itself.

These trips typically run half-day to full-day, combining hands-on activities — raptor banding, nest monitoring, track identification — with discussion of my research and the broader ecological questions it addresses.

I also mentor undergraduate students one-on-one in the lab, guiding them through independent research projects, data analysis in R, and scientific writing. Several former mentees have gone on to graduate programs in ecology and conservation.

  • K–12 Field Days

    Multi-school field days introducing students to wildlife tracking, ecology, and scientific careers.

  • Community Field Events

    Evening talks and guided field walks combining natural history with raptor ecology for general audiences.

  • Landowner & Rancher Engagement

    Workshops with local landowners to demonstrate coexistence strategies and share ecological monitoring data.

  • Undergraduate Mentorship

    One-on-one mentorship of 15+ undergraduates in field methods, R programming, and research design.
